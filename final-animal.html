Terrestrial animals are greatly affected by human pollution in a number of ways. The most obvious is through the degradation of their habitat, as pollution can destroy or alter ecosystems that animals rely on for survival. For example, deforestation and urbanization, which are driven by human activities, can lead to the destruction of animal habitats and the displacement of species. Pollution can also directly impact animal health, with exposure to pollutants in air, water, and soil leading to a range of health problems, including respiratory diseases, skin irritations, and reproductive issues. Additionally, pollution can also affect the food chain, with contaminants accumulating in plants and animals that are then consumed by higher-level predators, ultimately resulting in biomagnification and posing a threat to the health of entire ecosystems. In short, human pollution is a significant threat to terrestrial animals and their habitats, and urgent action is needed to mitigate its impacts and protect biodiversity.
