The following images show how humans have been affected by pollution.

Humans are greatly affected by mass pollution in a number of ways. The most obvious is through the degradation of the environment, which can lead to air, water, and soil pollution, all of which can have serious negative impacts on human health. For example, exposure to air pollution can cause respiratory problems, such as asthma, while water pollution can result in diseases like cholera and typhoid fever. Additionally, pollution can harm the food chain, leading to the consumption of contaminated food and affecting the health of both humans and wildlife. In the long term, exposure to pollution can also have a significant impact on mental health, leading to increased stress, anxiety, and depression. Ultimately, mass pollution is a major threat to human well-being and requires immediate action to mitigate its effects.
